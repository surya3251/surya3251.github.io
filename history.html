Origin of Chess

Chess originated in India around the 6th century AD. The earliest known form was called Chaturanga. It later spread to Persia, then the Arab world, and eventually Europe.

Evolution of Chess

  • 6th Century – Chaturanga in India
  • 7th Century – Shatranj in Persia
  • 10th Century – Spread through the Islamic world
  • 15th Century – Modern queen and bishop rules introduced
  • 19th Century – International tournaments began
  • 1886 – First official World Chess Championship

Chess in India

India is considered the birthplace of chess. The country has produced many grandmasters, including Viswanathan Anand, who became India's first World Chess Champion. India continues to produce strong young talents.

Interesting Facts

  • There are more possible chess games than atoms in the observable universe.
  • The longest official chess game lasted 269 moves.
  • Chess became an Olympic-recognized sport.
  • Computers now play stronger than humans.
  • Millions of games are played online every day.
