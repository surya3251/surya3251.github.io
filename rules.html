1. Objective of Chess

The objective of chess is to checkmate your opponent's king. A king is checkmated when it is under attack and cannot escape.

2. Starting Position

  • Each player has 16 pieces.
  • White always moves first.
  • Game is played on an 8×8 board.
  • Light square must be on each player's right side.

3. Check

A king is in check when an opponent's piece attacks it. You must remove the check immediately.

Ways to Escape Check

  • Move the king.
  • Capture the attacking piece.
  • Block the attack.

4. Checkmate

Checkmate happens when the king is in check and there is no legal move to escape. The player delivering checkmate wins the game.

5. Stalemate

If the player to move has no legal moves but the king is NOT in check, the game is a draw.

6. Castling

Conditions

  • King has never moved.
  • Rook has never moved.
  • No pieces between king and rook.
  • King cannot castle through check.
  • King cannot castle while in check.

7. Pawn Promotion

When a pawn reaches the last rank, it can become:

  • Queen
  • Rook
  • Bishop
  • Knight

Promotion to a queen is the most common choice.

8. En Passant

If a pawn moves two squares from its starting position and lands beside an enemy pawn, that enemy pawn may capture it as if it had moved only one square. This capture must be made immediately on the next move.

9. Draw Rules

  • Stalemate
  • Insufficient material
  • Threefold repetition
  • Fifty-move rule
  • Mutual agreement

10. Illegal Moves

  • Leaving your king in check.
  • Moving a piece incorrectly.
  • Castling through check.
  • Moving an opponent's piece.

11. Basic Tips

  1. Control the center.
  2. Develop knights before bishops.
  3. Castle early.
  4. Don't move the queen too early.
  5. Connect your rooks.
  6. Think before every move.
  7. Watch your opponent's threats.
