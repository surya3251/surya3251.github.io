What is a Chess Tactic?

A tactic is a short sequence of moves that gains an immediate advantage such as winning material or delivering checkmate.

1. Fork

Description

One piece attacks two or more enemy pieces at the same time. Knights are famous for forks.

Tip

Always look for knight forks on the king and queen.

2. Pin

A piece cannot move because a more valuable piece is behind it. Example: Bishop pins a knight to the king.

3. Skewer

Similar to a pin. The valuable piece moves away first, exposing a less valuable piece behind it.

4. Discovered Attack

One piece moves away, revealing an attack from another piece. Very powerful with bishops and rooks.

5. Double Check

Two pieces check the king simultaneously. The king must move because blocking or capturing usually won't stop both attacks.

6. Deflection

Force a defending piece away from an important square or piece.

7. Decoy

Lure an opponent's piece onto a bad square. Often used before delivering checkmate.

8. X-Ray Attack

Attack through another piece. Common with bishops, rooks and queens.

9. Zwischenzug (Intermediate Move)

Instead of making the expected move, play a stronger move first, often giving check or creating a threat.

10. Clearance Sacrifice

Sacrifice a piece to clear a square, rank, or diagonal for another attacking piece.

Best Tactical Tips

  1. Look for checks first.
  2. Then captures.
  3. Then threats.
  4. Calculate before moving.
  5. Watch for hanging pieces.
  6. Practice puzzles every day.
  7. Study master games.
  8. Don't rush your moves.
  9. Always ask what your opponent wants.
  10. Think two or three moves ahead.
