What is an Endgame?

The endgame begins when only a few pieces remain on the board. King activity becomes extremely important.

1. King Activity

Unlike the opening, the king becomes a strong attacking piece. Bring your king toward the center.

2. Opposition

Opposition is when two kings face each other with one square between them. The player who gains opposition often wins king and pawn endings.

3. Passed Pawn

A passed pawn has no opposing pawn in front of it on the same or adjacent files. Passed pawns are extremely valuable.

4. Outside Passed Pawn

A passed pawn far from the main action distracts the opponent's king. This often wins games.

5. King and Pawn Endgames

  • Opposition
  • Square Rule
  • Shouldering
  • Triangulation

6. Lucena Position

The most famous winning rook endgame. Build a bridge with your rook to promote the pawn.

7. Philidor Position

The most important drawing technique in rook endgames.

8. Queen Endgames

Queen endgames require accurate calculation because perpetual checks are common.

9. Bishop vs Knight

Bishops are usually stronger in open positions. Knights perform better in closed positions.

10. Zugzwang

A position where every legal move worsens your situation. Very common in king and pawn endgames.

Endgame Principles

  1. Activate your king.
  2. Push passed pawns.
  3. Cut off the opponent's king.
  4. Trade pieces, not pawns, when ahead.
  5. Know basic checkmates.
  6. Calculate carefully.
  7. Avoid unnecessary pawn moves.
  8. Keep your rook active.
  9. Create outside passed pawns.
  10. Study famous endgames.
