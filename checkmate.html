What is Checkmate?

Checkmate occurs when the king is under attack and there is no legal move to escape. The game ends immediately.

1. Queen + King Checkmate

The queen controls many squares while your king supports it. Force the enemy king to the edge and deliver checkmate.

Difficulty

⭐ Easy

2. Rook + King Checkmate

Push the enemy king to the edge using the rook while your king approaches. This is one of the first endgames every player should learn.

Difficulty

⭐⭐ Easy

3. Two Rooks (Ladder Mate)

Use both rooks to cut off ranks one after another until the king reaches the edge. Very simple and reliable.

4. Back Rank Mate

Occurs when the king is trapped behind its own pawns and a rook or queen delivers mate.

Tip

Always give your king an escape square.

5. Smothered Mate

The king is surrounded by its own pieces. A knight delivers the final checkmate.

6. Arabian Mate

A rook and knight work together near the corner to checkmate the king.

7. Anastasia's Mate

A rook traps the king on the edge while a knight blocks escape squares.

8. Boden's Mate

Two bishops attack along crossing diagonals to trap the king.

9. Bishop + Knight Mate

One of the most difficult basic checkmates. Requires accurate coordination between king, bishop and knight.

Difficulty

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Advanced

10. Fool's Mate

Fastest possible checkmate. Moves:

1.f3 e5 2.g4 Qh4#

11. Scholar's Mate

Popular beginner attack. Moves:

1.e4 e5 2.Bc4 Nc6 3.Qh5 Nf6 4.Qxf7#

Strong players usually defend against it easily.

Tips for Delivering Checkmate

  1. Keep your king active.
  2. Cut off escape squares.
  3. Coordinate your pieces.
  4. Look for forcing checks.
  5. Don't rush.
  6. Calculate carefully.
  7. Practice basic mates often.
  8. Learn common mating patterns.
